Public park land that Citi Field is built on is at risk of being turned into a casino. Under the guise of community development and engagement, the groundwork is being laid to hand over public land to a private group for private interests. Steve Cohen (estimated net worth of $13 billion & the current owner of the Mets) is working with big lobbying firms and is reportedly going to spend more than $700k to try make this happen.

Say NO to Steve's gamble to build a casino on public park land.

A Real Opportunity
We deserve better than a casino
The area around Citi Field is just asphalt used as a parking lot during baseball games but most of the year it sits vacant. A lot of people don’t know that this land including Citi Field itself is public park land. That means it belongs to you, me- all of us; it doesn’t belong solely to private interests. This area should be developed to serve the whole community. A casino will not do that. We deserve so much more. Community support for developing the land with green space, transit, making it a pleasant place to relax, walk or bike, and the nearby waterfront is being co-opted as community support for a casino. What the community supports must be delivered honestly and clearly to elected officials. These officials have the power to stop this casino proposal in its tracks, and build the meaningful things the community is asking for.
Our 60+ Acres, Not Steve Cohens
Remove the parking lot from the park
Vision
Better uses for 60+ acres than a casino and parking

Are the choices really between asphalt and a casino? Is asphalt all that this place can be? The groups advocating for building the casino would argue that a casino is a dealbreaker to build any of the things the community actually wants. This is a false choice. The land currently is park land, so why not build an actual park?

Mets-Willets Point is a busy area with both a 7 train and LIRR stop. With a growing population in nearby neighborhoods, and groundwork being done in Willets Point for a future neighborhood, we need to connect and support these communities with public transit.

This area currently is not only unpleasant to walk through at the moment, but also very dangerous. There is very little pedestrian infrastructure. In fact, one of the two connections between Corona and Flushing force pedestrians to cross the highway TWICE!

This area is a major thoroughfare to connect Eastern Queens to Western Queens. There is no cycling or micro mobility infrastructure. Fun fact: there’s no CitiBike at CitiField.

The Flushing Bay waterfront is in a very poor state. Deferred maintenance has made the promenade unsafe and unsightly. Elsewhere in the city, investments in development along the waterfront have produced beautiful waterfronts and economic viability that are visited and enjoyed by New Yorkers and visitors.

We shouldn’t need a casino to fund or justify these much improvements. This area of Queens has been neglected for far too long. These improvements and a casino SHOULD NOT be a bundled deal.

Community
Don't let Steve Cohen hijack the future of our community
The “visionaries” behind taking public park land to build a casino are neither transparent nor visionary with their vision. The public “vision meetings” and website fail to explicitly mention their true desire to build a casino. Instead, they overplay what the community is asking for: popular ideas like parks & greenspace, transit improvements, waterfront development, walkability, and micro mobility to shove through what they really want. They claim that they have overwhelming support from the community but they find it difficult to be honest and transparent with the community. The community will work with private interests when our goals align for everyone, but a proposal to build a casino aligns with a small group of individuals who would benefit disproportionately from it, while the community at large that would suffer greatly for it.

Dear [INSERT_OFFICIAL_NAME],

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the bill number #A05688 which would pave the way for Steven Cohen (through New Green Willets) to obtain public parkland for a casino development. As a concerned resident of Queens, I am troubled by the possibility of losing valuable parkland to private development and for a purpose that doesn’t suit the needs of all people in the community.

In addition to the loss of public parkland, I am deeply concerned about the negative impacts of a casino. This land sits between two mostly immigrant neighborhoods with high poverty levels. Studies have shown that casinos disproportionately harm low-income communities by increasing rates of gambling addiction, bankruptcy, and crime. As a representative who is committed to equity and social justice, I urge you to consider the potential social costs of allowing a casino to be built on public park land between such neighborhoods.

Furthermore, I would like to address the misleading support that Steve Cohen has drummed up for this project. While some members of the community may have expressed support for the development of the casino, it is important to note that Steve Cohen is using "visions" of waterfront development, greenspaces, walkability, transit improvements, and obscuring the casino aspect to gain community support.

Finally, I would like to reiterate that our parks are a vital resource for our community, providing much-needed green space for recreation and relaxation. Taking away this resource for the benefit of a single developer would be a grave injustice to the residents of Queens.

I urge you to stand with the people of Queens and reject this proposal to allow Steven Cohen to obtain public parkland for a casino development.
