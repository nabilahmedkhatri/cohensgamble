Steve Cohen (estimated net worth of $13.2 billion dollars) wants to use public land to build a casino. He has hired big lobbying firms and is reportedly going to spend more than $700k to make this happen. He is selling visions of community development underplaying: 1) that this land belongs to New Yorkers and 2) his true vision which is to build a casino. He claims that he is “building something great for all” when in reality Cohen is just betting on building something great for his wallet. Don’t bet on his gamble.

Say no to Cohen’s bid to build a casino on public park land.

Our 50 Acres, Not Steve Cohens
Remove the parking lot from the park
Vision
Better uses for 50 acres than a casino and parking

Steve Cohen is really trying hard to convince you that asphalt is all that this space can be. He's counting on most people not knowing that not only is this space currently public park land, but that Citi Field itself is built on public park land. So how about building an actual park?

Mets-Willets Point is a busy area with both a 7 train and LIRR stop. With a growing population in nearby neighborhoods, and groundwork being done in Willets Point for a future neighborhood, we need to connect and support these communities with public transit.

This area currently is not only unpleasant to walk through at the moment, but also very dangerous. There is very little pedestrian infrastructure. In fact, one of the two connections between Corona and Flushing force pedestrians to cross the highway TWICE!

This area is a major thoroughfare to connect Eastern Queens to Western Queens. There is no cycling or micromobility infrastructure. Fun fact: there’s no CitiBike at CitiField.

The Flushing Bay waterfront is in a very poor state. Deferred maintenance has made the promenade unsafe and unsightly. Elsewhere in the city, investments in development along the waterfront have produced beautiful waterfronts that are visited and enjoyed by New Yorkers and visitors.

We shouldn’t need a casino to fund or justify these much needed improvements. This area of Queens has been neglected for far too long. These improvements and a casino SHOULD NOT be a bundle deal.
